About DPWH

About DPWH

Our Mandate

The Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) functions as the primary engineering and construction arm of the government. It is responsibly tasked with the planning, design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructures such as national roads, strategic bridges, flood control systems, water resource development projects, and assorted public works systems aligned with national socio-economic objectives.

Vision

By 2040, DPWH is an excellent government agency, enabling a comfortable life for Filipinos through safe, reliable and resilient infrastructure.

Mission

To provide and manage quality infrastructure facilities and services responsive to the needs of the Filipino people in the pursuit of national development objectives.

Core Policy

We commit to provide quality, safe and environment-friendly public infrastructure facilities that will improve the life of Every Filipino. We commit to comply with all the requirements and to continually improve effectiveness and effeciency in serving the public. We endeavour to implement the RIGHT PROJECTS at the RIGHT COST determined through transparent and competitive bidding; with the RIGHT QUALITY, according to international standards; delivered RIGHT ON TIME through close monitoring of project implementation; and carried out by the RIGHT PEOPLE who are competent and committed to uphold the values of public service, integrity, professionalism, excellence, and teamwork.

History

Background

The development of the Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) into its present structure underwent a long process of evolution spanning a century of colorful and significant events in laying the groundwork for the physical foundation of the country.

The Department is considered as old as the Philippine government, its existence dates back to about four (4) centuries at the time of the Spanish colonial era. It emerged from its embryonic form in 1565 when the first settlement roads were constructed by forced labor. Fortresses then were connected by improved trails as supply lines and means of communications.

It was in 1868 when the Bureau of Public Works and Highways (Obras Publicas) and Bureau of Communications and Transportation (Communicationes y Meteologia) were organized under a civil engineer known as “Director General”.

Evolution Timeline Milestones

Year Evolution Milestones & Historical Content
1898
Department of War and Public Works

The Organic Decree issued by Gen. Emilio Aguinaldo establishing the Philippine Revolutionary Government created four (4) government departments among which was the Department of War and Public Works.
1902
Department of Commerce and Police

Bureau of Engineering and Construction of Public Works and Bureau of Architecture and Construction of Public Buildings - were created by Act. Nos 222 and 268 of the Philippine Commission and placed under the Department of Commerce and Police.
1931
The Department of Public Works and Communications

The Philippine Legislature passed on May 1 of that year Act No. 4007, renaming the Department of Commerce and Communications as Department of Public Works and Communications (DPWC).
1987
The Department of Public Works and Highways

By virtue of Executive Order No. 124, dated January 30, 1987, the agency assumed its contemporary classification as the Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH).

About the Logo

Symbolism and Interpretation

The official seal of the Department of Public Works and Highways represents corporate identity, engineering professionalism, and administrative focus towards nation building.

The Operational Gear Wheel

The outer cogwheel composition specifies continuous mechanical progress, technical growth, and unified forward motion. The structure counts exactly seventeen (17) gear teeth, explicitly mirroring the synchronized infrastructure alignment across all administrative regions of the Philippines.

The Doric Column and Expanding Highways

Positioned within the central gear frame layout, a classical Doric column is overlaid with two highway lanes converging onto a single infinity point horizon:

  • Doric Architectural Profile: Represents structural stability, design integrity, and engineering durability.
  • Converging Highway Framework: Symbolizes extensive physical integration, logistics networks, and smooth regional accessibility constructed for the public welfare.
